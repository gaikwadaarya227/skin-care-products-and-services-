MEDICINAL PLANTS

Medicinal plants are used globally for treating illnesses, boosting immunity,
and managing chronic conditions, with roughly 80%
of the world's population relying on them for healthcare.
They are utilized for their anti-inflammatory, antioxidant,
and antimicrobial properties in teas, powders, and extracts.

Medicinal plants contain phytochemicals—such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and polyphenols—that offer therapeutic benefits, with over 17,000 species used globally for treating ailments, strengthening immunity, and providing antioxidant support. Key examples include Aloe Vera (skin/digestion), Turmeric (immunity), and Ashwagandha (stress).
